## Step 1: Pick Your Dates Wisely

Mardi Gras isn't one dayâ€”it's a season. When you go matters.

### The Key Dates (2026)

| Day | Date | What's Happening |
|-----|------|------------------|
| Thursday | Feb 12 | Parades start rolling Uptown |
| Friday | Feb 13 | Krewe of Hermes, Le Krewe d'Etat |

### Which Days to Attend?

**Full Experience (5-6 days):** Thursday through Tuesday
- See the build-up, the peak, and the finale
- Expensive but worth it for the real experience

**Long Weekend (3-4 days):** Friday through Monday or Saturday through Tuesday
- Catch the major parades
- Miss some early ones but see the climax

**Quick Hit (2-3 days):** Saturday through Monday
- Endymion, Bacchus, and Lundi Gras
- Mardi Gras day is Tuesdayâ€”decide if you can extend

**Mardi Gras Day Only:** Tuesday
- You'll see the end but miss the build-up
- Better than nothing, but not the full experience

### Our Recommendation for Groups

**Arrive Friday, Leave Wednesday.** This gives you:
- Time to settle in before the chaos
- All the major parades
- Mardi Gras day
- A recovery day before traveling

## Step 6: Parade Strategy for Groups

### Picking Your Spot

**Uptown Route (St. Charles Ave):**
- More space, family-friendly
- Best for: daytime parades, seeing the full route
- Stake out spots early for big parades

**Downtown Route (Canal St):**
- More crowded, more intense
- Best for: night parades, being in the action
- Harder to keep groups together

**Mid-City (Endymion route):**
- Local favorite
- Best for: Endymion specifically
- Less touristy feel

### Group Parade Protocol

1. **Pick a meeting spot** â€” Specific corner, specific side of street
2. **Arrive 2-3 hours early** for big parades (Endymion, Bacchus, Zulu)
3. **Bring supplies:**
   - Folding chairs
   - Cooler with drinks and snacks
   - Bags for catching throws
   - Portable phone chargers
   - Rain gear (just in case)

4. **Set a rally point** â€” If anyone gets separated, where do they go?
5. **Share locations** â€” Everyone shares their phone location for the weekend

### The Buddy System

Mardi Gras crowds are dense. Set rules:
- No one leaves alone
- Bathroom trips in pairs minimum
- Set check-in times if you split up
- Everyone knows the house address by heart

## Step 7: House Rules for Mardi Gras

### Survival Basics

**Hydration Station:** Stock the house with:
- Cases of water
- Gatorade/electrolytes
- Pedialyte (no shame)

**Food Stockpile:**
- Breakfast supplies (eggs, bacon, bread)
- Snacks (chips, fruit, protein bars)
- Late-night food (frozen pizza, easy stuff)

**Recovery Zone:**
- Designate quiet hours (3am-10am)
- Have a "chill room" for people who need a break
- First aid kit with basics + aspirin/ibuprofen

### Rotation Schedule

Not everyone needs to do every parade. Create a rough schedule:

```
FRIDAY
======
Day parade: Full crew
Night parade: Most people
Late night: Whoever's standing

SATURDAY (Endymion)
===================
Recovery morning: Sleep in
Pre-parade: Pool/house hang
Endymion: ALL HANDS ON DECK
After: Optional French Quarter

SUNDAY (Bacchus)
================
Brunch crew: Early risers
Pool crew: Recovery mode
Bacchus: Full crew rallies
```

Let people opt out. Forcing exhausted people to parade isn't fun for anyone.

## Step 8: Transportation During Mardi Gras

### The Reality

- Many streets are **closed** for parades
- Ubers are **expensive** and **slow**
- Traffic is **nightmarish**

### Solutions

**Walk:** If you're staying Uptown or in the Quarter, walk to parades.

**Bike:** Rent bikes for getting around. Faster than cars during Mardi Gras.

**Streetcar:** The St. Charles line runs along the parade route. Slow but scenic.

**Rideshare Strategy:**
- Order well before you need to leave
- Set pickup points away from parade routes
- Expect surge pricing (2-3x normal)
- Budget $50-100/person for the week

**Best approach:** Stay somewhere walkable to the parades you care most about.

## Step 9: What Goes Wrong

### Someone Gets Lost

- **Share phone locations** with the whole group before day one
- Set a **rally point** at the house if all else fails
- **Text, don't call** â€” texts get through when calls don't

### Someone Goes Too Hard

- Mardi Gras is a marathon, not a sprint
- Pace yourselvesâ€”it's multiple days
- Keep water everywhere
- Know the signs of heat exhaustion and alcohol poisoning

### Weather

- Check the forecast, but know it can change
- Pack layers and rain gear
- Parades roll rain or shine (mostly)
- Cold snaps happenâ€”bring a jacket

### House Issues

- Get the property manager's number
- Know where the breaker box is
- Have plungers in every bathroom
- Report damage immediately
